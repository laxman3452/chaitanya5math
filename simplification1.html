Lesson Plan Overview

Duration: 80 Minutes | Subject: Mathematics

Learning Objectives

  • Translate real-life situations into mathematical statements.
  • Recall the four basic arithmetic operations.
  • Understand that multiplication and division are solved before addition and subtraction.
  • Solve simple mixed-operation expressions correctly.

Required Materials

Projector/Display, Whiteboard & colored markers, Practice Worksheets, Student Notebooks.

PHASE 1: WARM-UP & MOTIVATION (15 MIN)

Activity 1: Story Math

Read the scenarios to the class. Have students write the mathematical expression in their notebooks, then reveal the answer blocks below to check.

Scenario A

"Your mother gave you 15 chocolates. Your father gave you 5 more. Then, you gave 7 chocolates to your sister. How many chocolates remain?"

15 + 5 - 7 = 13

Scenario B

"There are 20 birds on a tree. 15 more birds join them. Later, 13 birds fly away. How many birds are left?"

20 + 15 - 13 = 22

Discussion Corner

Ask Students:
  • Which operation comes first?
  • Does the order matter when only addition and subtraction are present?
Teacher Note: For expressions containing only addition and subtraction, always solve sequentially from left to right.
PHASE 2: INTRODUCTION (20 MIN)

Activity 2: Operation Detective

Write the following problem on the physical board or project it here:

10 × 7 - 37

Ask the detectives: Which operation should be solved first? Why?

The Golden Rule of Order

1
Multiplication (×) and Division (÷) First
2
Addition (+) and Subtraction (-) Last

⚠️ Always move across the expression from Left to Right!

PHASE 2: GUIDED STEP-BY-STEP

Guided Examples

Walk through these problems with the class. Click each step to show the dynamic conversion of the expression.

Guided Example 1: 55 ÷ 11 + 5 × 2

Step 1: Solve Division (55 ÷ 11)
Step 2: Solve Multiplication (5 × 2)
Step 3: Solve Final Addition
55 ÷ 11 + 5 × 2 → ... → ...

Guided Example 2: 17 - 9 ÷ 3 × 2

*Notice: Division and Multiplication are tied. Run them left to right!*

Step 1: Division First (9 ÷ 3)
Step 2: Multiplication Next (3 × 2)
Step 3: Final Subtraction
17 - 9 ÷ 3 × 2 → ... → ...
PHASE 3: GROUP ACTIVITY (20 MIN)

Activity 3: Build a Story

Divide students into groups. Challenge them to match expressions to real-life math situations or create their own.

Group A Assignment

11 + 2 - 4

Sample Target Story: "Rabin had 11 pencils. His friend gave him 2 more pencils, but he lost 4 pencils."

Group B Assignment

37 - 15 + 8

Sample Target Story: "A baker had 37 cupcakes. He sold 15 cupcakes and later baked 8 more."

Activity 4: Human Number Line

Setup: Hand out structural numeric cards and operation symbol tags to selected students. Have them line up at the front of the classroom and physically step forward out of the line when it is their operation's turn to execute!

PHASE 4: GUIDED PRACTICE (15 MIN)

Interactive Board Practice

Call up students to solve these live, or have them write in their copies and check answers dynamically step-by-step.

  • 1) 5 × 6 + 4
  • 2) 24 ÷ 6 + 7
  • 3) 18 - 3 × 2
  • 4) 40 ÷ 5 × 2
